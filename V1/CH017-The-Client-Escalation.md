# CH017: The Client Escalation

The video call was projected on the big screen. The client, a VP from the banking partner, was red-faced and shouting.

"This is unacceptable! We were promised stability, and we're seeing 500 errors during peak hours! If this isn't fixed by Monday, we are pulling the contract!"

The volume was turned up too high. The shouting echoed in the small room.

Mara was stiff, her jaw clenched. Jonah was trying to interrupt, his voice high and defensive. "But if you look at the SLA—"

"I don't care about the SLA! I care about my customers!"

Elara sat at the end of the table. Her heart was hammering. The instinct to defend, to explain, to apologize was overwhelming. She could feel the "Must fix it" alarm blaring in her head.

*Ping.*

**Nexus:** *High pressure system detected.*

**Elara:** *He's furious. I need to calm him down.*

**Nexus:** *You cannot control the weather. You can only build a shelter.*

**Nexus:** *Experiment 017.*
**Nexus:** *Context:* External chaos.
**Nexus:** *Inquiry:* To lead is to hold the state.
**Nexus:** *Task:* Do not match the frequency. Drop your shoulders. Slow your breath. Be the ground.

Elara looked at the shouting man. She looked at Mara and Jonah, who were vibrating with the same frantic energy.

She exhaled. She consciously dropped her shoulders. She uncrossed her legs and planted her feet flat on the floor. She forced her breathing to slow down. *In... two... three... Out... two... three...*

She didn't speak. She just sat there, anchoring herself in the chair.

The client ran out of breath. "Well? What do you have to say?"

The silence stretched. It wasn't an empty silence. It was a heavy, grounded silence. Elara held it for two seconds longer than was comfortable.

"We hear you," Elara said. Her voice was an octave lower than usual, slow and deliberate. "The error rate is unacceptable. We are not hiding behind the SLA."

She paused. The client blinked. He had expected a fight. He got a wall.

"We are investigating the root cause," she continued, keeping the rhythm steady. "We will have a remediation plan on your desk by 4 PM. We will not go home until the stability is verified."

The VP stared at her. The red began to fade from his face. Her calm was contagious. He couldn't keep shouting at a rock.

"4 PM," he grunted. "It better be good."

"It will be thorough," Elara corrected gently.

The call ended. The room was quiet.

Mara looked at Elara, her eyes wide. "How did you do that?"

"I didn't do anything," Elara said, picking up her water glass. Her hand was shaking slightly, but her voice was still steady. "I just didn't let him pull me into the storm."

***

Elara sat on the balcony, wrapped in a blanket. The wind was whipping around the building, rattling the railing.

Patch sat on the ledge, watching the leaves swirl in the gale. The wind ruffled his fur, pushing him off balance.

He didn't hiss at the wind. He didn't try to catch the leaves. He just crouched lower, flattening his body against the concrete, turning himself into a heavy, immovable shape.

"Low center of gravity," Elara noted, sipping her tea.

Patch narrowed his eyes against the gust, waiting it out. He knew he couldn't stop the wind. He just had to be heavier than it.
